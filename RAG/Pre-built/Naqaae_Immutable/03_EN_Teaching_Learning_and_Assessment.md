# Standard (3): Teaching, Learning and Assessment — 8 Indicators
## Programmatic Accreditation Standards — NAQAAE 2022 Amended Edition

> **Note:** This standard retained the same number of indicators (8) in the amended edition; detailed guidance notes were added for each indicator.

---

### Indicator 3-1: Teaching and Learning Methods
The program applies diverse teaching and learning methods that achieve the intended learning outcomes of the courses.

**Guidance Notes:**
- The appropriateness of the theoretical teaching and learning methods actually applied for imparting the required knowledge to students (as evidenced by published timetables or course files) and their alignment with what is stated in the specifications and matrices
- The appropriateness and effectiveness of practical training in achieving the skills required by graduates (as evidenced by published timetables or course files)
- The appropriateness of methods used for distance/online learning (if applicable)

---

### Indicator 3-2: Active Learning and Skills Development
Teaching and learning methods are applied that encourage students to take an active role in their learning process and support self-directed learning, higher-order thinking skills, employability skills, and entrepreneurship.

**Guidance Notes:**
- Identifying the practices actually applied through teaching and learning methods or assessment activities

---

### Indicator 3-3: Field Training
Field training is conducted in collaboration with relevant entities and appropriate employers to ensure students' professional development, through a clear, approved and published plan, mechanisms and procedures that organise the training process, supervision and evaluation.

**Guidance Notes:**
- Existence of an institutional mechanism / handbook for managing and organising field training
- The appropriateness of the field training and its allocated duration for achieving the course/program ILOs and competencies
- The diversity of field training sites and the appropriateness of supervision and evaluation mechanisms at those sites
- Identifying the methods used to evaluate field training
- Incorporation of field training results in student assessment (marks allocated to field training)
- Identifying the methods for measuring the effectiveness of field training in equipping students with the required skills, and the impact of results analysis on the continuous improvement of the training

---

### Indicator 3-4: Diversity of Student Assessment Methods
Students are assessed using diverse methods (written, practical, oral, clinical, field-based, projects and assignments, case studies, portfolios, and others), and the marks allocated to the different assessment methods are balanced.

**Guidance Notes:**
- The appropriateness of the student assessment methods applied in each course (written exams / practical exams / assignments and tasks / graduation projects / clinical exams / etc.), in terms of their coverage of all planned course ILOs (review of the course file and its sample of different assessment methods)
- The appropriateness and balance of marks allocated to each assessment method according to the knowledge and skills being measured
- The appropriateness of the timing of different assessments to achieve continuous assessment

---

### Indicator 3-5: Examination Setting Mechanism
The program has an approved mechanism for setting the various examinations, and procedures for verifying that they cover the scientific content of the courses and that they are aligned with and satisfy the ILOs/competencies of the educational program and the courses.

**Guidance Notes:**
- Identifying the rules for forming examination committees with examples of the procedures and mechanisms followed
- Identifying the means of verifying the alignment of different examinations (written/practical/clinical/assignments or tasks/projects, etc.) with the target learning outcomes/competencies, such as the use of specification tables (blueprints), examination paper evaluation, etc.
- The role of the faculty's measurement and evaluation unit (if applicable)

---

### Indicator 3-6: Assessment Fairness and Confidentiality
The program has approved and published mechanisms to ensure the fairness of student assessment through: examination management systems and procedures, examination committee working systems, documentation of examination results, and rules for ensuring confidentiality and fairness.

**Guidance Notes:**
- Activation of procedures for: securing examinations and ensuring their confidentiality when copied and distributed, monitoring students and preventing cheating (including electronic examinations where applicable)
- The appropriateness of the rules for forming and defining the tasks of examination control committees (central and internal, where applicable)
- Activation of mechanisms to ensure the fairness of the examination system such as: rules for oral examinations — avoidance of conflicts of interest with faculty and teaching assistants — fairness of marking (more than one marker per paper, use of answer models and rubrics, etc.) — accuracy of grade recording — auditing and review — etc.
- The appropriateness of the system for documenting all examination results and keeping results secured (also in the case of electronic assessment methods where applicable)

---

### Indicator 3-7: Using Assessment Results for Development
Student assessment results are analysed, discussed in the relevant councils and committees, and used to develop the program.

**Guidance Notes:**
- Reports analysing student examination results at different levels (courses / year groups)
- Preparation of annual statistics on the development of pass rates at different levels (for a number of years equal to the duration of the educational program)
- Presentation and discussion of results in the relevant councils, and the adoption of corrective measures

---

### Indicator 3-8: Feedback to Students
Students are provided with feedback on their performance in learning and assessment activities in a manner that supports their learning.

**Guidance Notes:**
- The use of formative examinations as part of teaching and learning activities (including providing students with feedback during their skills training)
- Informing students of the results of different assessments (written exams / practical exams / assignments and tasks / graduation projects / clinical exams / etc.) through appropriate means and at appropriate times, and discussing results with them to support their learning
