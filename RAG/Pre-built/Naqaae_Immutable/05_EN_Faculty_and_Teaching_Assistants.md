# Standard (5): Faculty and Teaching Assistants — 6 Indicators (Amended)
## Programmatic Accreditation Standards — NAQAAE 2022 Amended Edition

---

### Indicator 5-1: Number of Faculty Members
The program has a sufficient number of faculty members to enable effective delivery of educational activities so as to ensure teaching quality and accreditation, and to allow an appropriate workload in accordance with applicable regulations and laws.

**Guidance Notes:**
- The number of faculty members is sufficient to cover the educational requirements of the program according to the Authority's benchmark standards, with reference to the balance among different academic ranks
- The teaching workload is appropriate relative to the actual approved and published timetables, in a way that is compatible with academic advising and support duties, administrative, research and community activities, and continuous professional development activities

---

### Indicator 5-2: Number of Teaching Assistants
The program has a sufficient number of teaching assistants to enable effective delivery of educational activities in accordance with the NAQAAE reference benchmarks for ensuring teaching quality and accreditation, and to allow an appropriate workload in accordance with applicable regulations and laws.

**Guidance Notes:**
- The number of teaching assistants is sufficient to cover the educational requirements of the program according to the Authority's benchmark standards
- The teaching workload is appropriate relative to the actual approved and published timetables, in a way that is compatible with the rules for academic leave and administrative duties (with reference to the average time taken to obtain the academic degree as an indicator of workload appropriateness)

---

### Indicator 5-3: Qualifications and Competencies of Faculty
The qualifications, experience and competencies of the program's faculty members and teaching assistants are appropriate for the courses they teach in the program.

**Guidance Notes:**
- The appropriateness of the scientific specialisations of faculty members and teaching assistants for the courses they teach, as evidenced by CVs and timetables
- The diversity of academic schools represented among the faculty

---

### Indicator 5-4: Faculty Selection Criteria
Approved and published criteria and fair, transparent procedures exist for selecting faculty members and teaching assistants in a manner that ensures attracting qualified talent. Surplus or shortage cases are identified periodically and the formal, approved procedures are applied to address them.

**Guidance Notes:**
- Identifying the selection criteria and procedures, the means of publishing them, and the date of their approval — with examples of actual application such as advertisements and interviews (applicable only in: private universities, special programmes at public universities, and also rules for appointing research assistants)
- The procedures followed for attracting/retaining qualified talent
- Identifying the procedures for handling shortages/surpluses and examples of their activation (including alignment with research assistant appointment plans)
- The effectiveness of these procedures over the past three years as evidenced by changes in faculty staffing ratios

---

### Indicator 5-5: Continuous Professional Development
Faculty members and teaching assistants participate regularly in continuous professional development activities to ensure they keep pace with current trends in teaching, learning and assessment, scientific research, and professional developments in the specialisation. This is documented in the program's databases.

**Guidance Notes:**
- Identifying the type and number of training courses obtained by the program's faculty members and teaching assistants over at least the past three years, and the number and percentage of those trained from each category relative to the total

---

### Indicator 5-6: Research and Community Activity of Faculty
Faculty members participate in research activities (supervision of theses / local and international publication / local and international conferences and seminars / research projects / scholarships and research grants / etc.) and/or in community and professional activities, and this is documented in the databases.

**Guidance Notes:**
- A record of the research activity of faculty members (total theses, scientific papers, research projects, conferences and seminars, intellectual/artistic output, innovations, etc.) for the past three years — and/or the community and professional activities of faculty members to demonstrate the extent of engagement with the labour market sectors relevant to the program

---

> **Note:** The original 2022 edition includes 7 indicators, additionally covering:
> - Original 5-7: A periodic performance evaluation of the program's faculty members and teaching assistants exists according to approved and published criteria and procedures. They are informed of and discussed with the evaluation results, and mechanisms exist to encourage performance improvement and address shortcomings
