# Standard (2): Program Design — 4 Indicators (Amended)
## Programmatic Accreditation Standards — NAQAAE 2022 Amended Edition

---

### Indicator 2-1: Academic Reference Standards
The program adopts the National Academic Reference Standards (NARS) or any other reference standards, through approved procedures via formal councils, and all relevant stakeholders are made aware of the adopted standards.

**Guidance Notes:**
- Identifying the procedures by which the program adopts NARS or Alternative Reference Standards (ARS), and the means of raising awareness among all relevant stakeholders
- In cases where the program adopts ARS: demonstrating the appropriateness of the adopted standards and their alignment with the NARS of the relevant sector, and their inclusion of the additional knowledge and skills expected of the program's graduates (ARS–NARS alignment matrix)

---

### Indicator 2-2: Program Structure
The program structure achieves: integration among its components (courses and activities that are consistent with one another and reflect current scientific and professional developments in the field), balance between theoretical and applied content (practical/field training), and alignment between general requirements and specialisation requirements, thereby fulfilling the program's objectives.

**Guidance Notes:**
- The appropriateness of the program structure components and the extent to which they achieve the adopted standards: proportion of different sciences — courses and their logical sequencing — the practical/applied component — adequacy of field training — etc.
- The programme regulations are clear and complete in terms of: admission rules, duration and language of study, name of the degree awarded, courses at each level, assessment methods with their timing and marks, etc.
- When the regulations are updated, the reasons for the update and the changes made to the program structure are documented

---

### Indicator 2-3: Program Specification
The program has an approved and published specification in accordance with its approved regulations.

**Guidance Notes — Quality and completeness of the program specification:**
- Basic data
- Clear Program Aims linked to the program's mission
- Program Intended Learning Outcomes (ILOs)
- Program ILOs / Adopted standards matrix — showing the extent to which the ILOs achieve the adopted standards (not required when the competencies or outcomes of the academic standards are used directly as the program's ILOs)
- Program courses with the credit hours/points of each course
- Courses / Program ILOs matrix — showing the adequacy and balance of course distribution against the program ILOs (if the academic standards' competencies serve as the program ILOs, the matrix is: Courses / Adopted Standards)
- Teaching, learning and assessment strategies or methods
- Teaching, learning and assessment strategies/methods / Program ILOs matrix — showing the alignment of strategies/methods with the ILOs to be achieved
- Entry requirements and the rules governing program completion and graduation
- Rules governing field training
- Program evaluation methods
- Date of approval by the relevant councils

---

### Indicator 2-4: Course Specifications
The program's courses have approved and published specifications in accordance with its approved regulations.

**Guidance Notes — Quality and completeness of course specifications:**
- Basic course data
- Course objectives
- Course ILOs: clear and measurable — achieving the competencies/program outcomes (as defined in the Courses/Program ILOs matrix)
- Scientific content targeted for teaching, the contact hours allocated per topic, its alignment with the specified learning outcomes, and its currency with recent developments in the field
- Content of the practical component/lab experiments/training or field training
- Teaching, learning and assessment methods and their alignment with the course ILOs (Teaching, Learning and Assessment Methods / Course ILOs matrix)
- Scientific references
- Date of approval by the relevant councils

---

> **Note:** The original 2022 edition includes 8 indicators, additionally covering:
> - Original 2-2: The program has clear Program Aims linked to its mission and consistent with the graduate profile defined by the adopted academic standards
> - Original 2-4: The program has an approved and published teaching, learning and assessment strategy, consistent with the adopted academic standards and supporting the achievement of the program's ILOs, reviewed periodically based on continuous evaluation results
> - Original 2-6: The program has matrices showing the alignment and consistency of: Program ILOs with the adopted academic standards — Program ILOs with courses — Program ILOs with teaching, learning and assessment methods
> - Original 2-8: The program is designed/developed with the participation of all relevant stakeholders/beneficiaries according to approved procedures that take into account: program mission and objectives — labour market requirements — comparable programs in the specialisation
