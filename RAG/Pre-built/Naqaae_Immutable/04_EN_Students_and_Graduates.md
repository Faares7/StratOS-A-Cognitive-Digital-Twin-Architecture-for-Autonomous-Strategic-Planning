# Standard (4): Students and Graduates — 3 Indicators (Amended)
## Programmatic Accreditation Standards — NAQAAE 2022 Amended Edition

---

### Indicator 4-1: Academic Support for Students
An announced and activated academic support system exists for all students enrolled in the program (academic advising / academic mentoring), comprising mechanisms and procedures for monitoring their academic progress, identifying and supporting high-achieving, gifted and struggling students (including students with special needs). Its effectiveness is evaluated periodically and developed in light of evaluation results.

**Guidance Notes:**
- Identifying the academic support system for students enrolled in the program and the means of announcing it
- The appropriateness of the system's application and its effectiveness in monitoring students' academic progress in the program
- Identifying the means for evaluating the effectiveness of academic advising and developing it in light of evaluation results, with examples demonstrating that such evaluation is activated and periodic
- The appropriateness of the mechanisms applied for identifying and supporting high-achieving students (e.g. scholarships, awards and certificates of appreciation, financial rewards, academic mentoring, etc.), struggling students (e.g. reducing course loads in the credit-hour system, additional instructional sessions, etc.) and gifted students (e.g. artistic and sports performances, cultural competitions, etc.)

---

### Indicator 4-2: Student Activities and Career Guidance
The program encourages students to participate in diverse student activities that provide opportunities for early and ongoing exposure to acquire research and community experience. Data on the number of participating students is available, and the program provides career guidance and counselling services to students.

**Guidance Notes:**
- Examples of the program's students participating in various student activities, competitions and awards in which they excelled at university, national and international levels
- Examples of student participation in scientific research, innovation and community service activities
- Identifying the procedures for providing counselling and career guidance to students, such as: the number of times students are advised — the person responsible for providing counselling (e.g. program faculty members, the department, faculty or university advisor) — regularity of holding career fairs — career guidance programmes offered for obtaining a professional licence, where applicable — etc.

---

### Indicator 4-3: Graduate Follow-up
Activated procedures exist for communication with graduates, supporting them and monitoring their professional progress.

**Guidance Notes:**
- Identifying the number of graduates and graduation rates from the program over the past five years
- Identifying the means of communication with the program's graduates
- Examples of graduates' participation in program activities

---

> **Note:** The original 2022 edition includes 7 indicators, additionally covering:
> - Original 4-1: Clear, approved, fair and published policies and rules are applied for admission to the program, transfer to or from it, and course registration, with data on the numbers of newly admitted students and transfer rates available annually and analysed
> - Original 4-2: The program applies effective procedures for supporting incoming international students and maintains data on the development of their numbers over the past five years (where applicable)
> - Original 4-5: Students are represented in the councils and committees relevant to the program in a manner appropriate to the program's nature and according to approved and published rules
> - Original 4-6: A graduate database exists for the program, updated periodically
> - Original 4-7: The program provides professional development resources and continuing education activities for program graduates to keep pace with developments and changes in the labour market
