# Standard (1): Program Mission and Management — 4 Indicators (Amended)
## Programmatic Accreditation Standards — NAQAAE 2022 Amended Edition

---

### Indicator 1-1: Program Mission
The program has a clear, approved, and published mission that is consistent with the institution's mission and community needs. The relevant stakeholders participated in its development, and it is reviewed and updated periodically to keep pace with developments.

**Guidance Notes:**
- The appropriateness of the mission statement and its alignment with the institution's mission
- Identifying the date of approval by the relevant governing councils
- Identifying the means of publication and announcement inside and outside the institution
- Identifying the parties involved — inside and outside the institution — who participated in developing/discussing/drafting the mission
- Identifying the body responsible for updating the mission, the periodicity of its review, and the review mechanisms/procedures

---

### Indicator 1-2: Program Leadership
The program has qualified leadership selected according to approved and announced criteria, whose performance is evaluated periodically. The program is managed in collaboration with the relevant committees and councils with defined mandates according to the institution's organizational structure.

**Guidance Notes:**
- Identifying the program leader / coordinator (deputy dean, department head, academic officer, or equivalent) and defining their functional responsibilities
- Identifying the coordinator selection criteria, the date of their approval, the means of announcing them, and the extent to which they are applied (with examples demonstrating objectivity and how they are applied, as evidenced by how the current coordinator was selected)
- Identifying performance measurement indicators for the program leader/coordinator and their objectivity
- Identifying the extent of activation and periodicity of evaluation (at least one evaluation for the last three years, with examples from the most recent evaluation results)
- Identifying the governing councils and committees of the program according to the institution's organizational structure

---

### Indicator 1-3: Marketing and Visibility
The program uses diverse means to make comprehensive information about it available, highlighting its competitive features and working to market it locally, regionally and internationally.

**Guidance Notes:**
- The program has a website within the institution's website, updated periodically
- It contains accurate and comprehensive data and information about the program, aiming to market it and clarify its distinctiveness from other programs in the same institution and in comparable institutions elsewhere (attracting incoming students, student exchange, etc.)

---

### Indicator 1-4: International Agreements and Partnerships
Where agreements or partnerships exist between the program and international institutions, the roles and responsibilities of both parties are defined and the benefits are monitored.

**Guidance Notes:**
- Applies only in the case of joint programs or programs that have international agreements

---

> **Note:** The original 2022 edition includes 8 indicators, additionally covering:
> - Original 1-3: The program has a sufficient number of qualified specialists to operate its physical resources and provide technical and IT support, with their capacities developed through continuous professional development programs and their performance evaluated periodically
> - Original 1-4: The program applies institutional policies and plans for dealing with educational problems and crises/disasters (student overcrowding, low enrolment, private tutoring, poor attendance, university textbooks, virtual/digital infrastructure, pandemics, examination leaks, cheating, etc.)
> - Original 1-5: The program applies the regulations and policies approved by the institution regarding: adherence to professional ethics and academic integrity — research ethics — intellectual property and publishing rights — ensuring fairness and non-discrimination — complaints and grievances — accountability
> - Original 1-7: Community stakeholders participate in the program's councils, committees and activities
