# NARS for Operations Research and Decision Support (OR/DS)
## NAQAAE | Computing and Information, 1st Edition, October 2010

> Standards below are **in addition to** the General Computing and Information NARS.

---

## Introduction

Operations Research (OR) and Decision Support (DS) emphasize **optimizing organizational and system performance** using advanced analytical methods to help make better decisions.

**Industry Applications:** Healthcare, logistics, financial services, finance, manufacturing, marketing, retail, spare parts manufacturing, and more.

**Program Goal:** To produce a graduate capable of analyzing and developing models and supporting complex decisions in different systems and operations at all managerial levels — playing an effective role in decision formulation in corporate and wider societal settings, identifying salient parameters necessary for decision-making, and utilizing information and techniques from diverse fields.

The program aims to produce a **problem solver** with a sufficient grasp of fundamental techniques and approaches to decision support and a broad intellectual outlook for contributing in a wide variety of settings.

---

## Graduate Attributes

After completing the OR/DS program, the graduate should be able to:

1. Exhibit the fundamental models of operational research and their computational methods
2. Develop the algorithmic techniques for combinatorial optimization problems
3. Practice the most important algorithmic techniques and their mathematical theory to implement both linear and non-linear optimization algorithms
4. Demonstrate strong skills in the mathematical foundation of decision-making methods
5. Demonstrate knowledge of the theoretical foundation of intelligent systems
6. Understand the mathematical foundation of analytical methods used in artificial intelligence tools
7. Demonstrate sufficient knowledge about methods of production control of computer or industrial systems
8. Deal with different stochastic programming models and their solution methods
9. Exhibit the fundamentals of system approach and system thinking
10. Exhibit the fundamentals of decision support based on computer technology
11. Deepen the analysis skills to reach concrete alternatives
12. Report the results of analysis
13. Continue viewing and using new ideas in future problems

---

## 1. Knowledge and Understanding

The OR/DS graduate should additionally acquire knowledge and understanding of:

1. Different types of models with complex operations including quantitative and qualitative factors
2. How those models are used to support and facilitate decision-making
3. Implementing appropriate technologies in the design and operation of organizations and enterprises
4. The different types and application domains of Decision Support Systems (DSS)
5. Decision-making and support through traditional and technological rationalization
6. Various approaches to solve managerial problems such as Production Management, Operations Management, Inventory Management, Project Management and Supply Chain Management
7. Key concepts of Game Theory, Decision Theory and Queuing Theory and the role of Operations Research
8. Merging both models and optimization approaches with Neural Computation, Evolutionary Computation, etc. to enhance solution processes

---

## 2. Intellectual Skills

The OR/DS graduate should additionally be able to:

1. View any real-life problem as a system and a part of a larger system
2. Summarize and visualize the problem using OR/MS methodology
3. Formulate models for any real-life problems using appropriate modeling techniques
4. Solve the model with appropriate techniques to get the solution (decision)
5. Define, analyze and solve complex problems within and between enterprises

---

## 3. Professional and Practical Skills

The OR/DS graduate should additionally be able to:

1. Develop models using different modeling techniques
2. Develop and use DS/MS/OR software packages
3. Study, analyze, evaluate and improve dynamic systems at any stage of the decision-making process
4. Utilize and adopt advanced technology (computer and information systems) to serve and support decision-making
5. Design a system, component or process to meet desired needs
