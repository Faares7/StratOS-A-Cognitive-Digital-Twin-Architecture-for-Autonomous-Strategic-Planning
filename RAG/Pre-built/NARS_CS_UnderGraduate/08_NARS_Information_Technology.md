# NARS for Information Technology (IT)
## NAQAAE | Computing and Information, 1st Edition, October 2010

> Standards below are **in addition to** the General Computing and Information NARS.

---

## Introduction

Organizations of every kind are dependent on information technology. They need appropriate systems in place that work properly, are secure, and are upgraded, maintained, and replaced as appropriate.

**IT vs. IS:** While both IT and IS include focus on software and hardware, IT's emphasis is on the **technology itself** more than on the information it conveys. IT is a new and rapidly growing field that started as a grassroots response to practical, everyday needs of business and other organizations.

**IT focuses on:**
- Ensuring the organization's infrastructure is appropriate and reliable
- Using computing to meet the needs of technology-dependent organizations
- Preparing graduates to meet the computer technology needs of business, government, healthcare, schools, and other kinds of organizations

**IT in academic terms:** Focuses on meeting the needs of users within an organizational and societal context through the **selection, creation, application, integration and administration** of computing technologies.

---

## Graduate Attributes

An IT graduate must acquire a skill set to successfully perform integrative tasks, including:

1. Knowledge of computing and mathematics appropriate to the discipline
2. Analyze a problem, and identify and define the computing requirements appropriate to its solution
3. Design, implement, and evaluate a computer-based system, process, component, or program to meet desired needs
4. Demonstrate independent critical thinking and problem solving skills; function effectively on a team
5. Understanding of professional, ethical, legal, security and social issues and responsibilities
6. Communicate effectively with a range of audiences
7. Analyze the local and global impact of computing on individuals, organizations, and society
8. Recognition of the need for and ability to engage in continuing professional development
9. Use current techniques, skills, and tools necessary for IT practice and in the creation of an effective project plan
10. Use and apply current technical concepts and practices in the core IT subjects
11. Identify and analyze user needs and take them into account in the selection, creation, evaluation and administration of computer-based systems
12. Address IT problems of organizations or individuals
13. Effectively integrate IT-based solutions into the user environment
14. Understand best practices and standards and their application

---

## 1. Knowledge and Understanding

The IT graduate should additionally acquire knowledge and understanding of:

1. Fundamental principles of core computing
2. Strong knowledge of fundamentals of programming, construction of computer-based systems, data structures and algorithms, software engineering techniques and information retrieval
3. Deeper understanding of aspects: multimedia, computer and communication networks, data mining and knowledge discovery, information storage and retrieval systems, mobile communication systems, pattern recognition, AI, cryptography and network security
4. Technologies for the design, development and management of database systems, systems analysis and design, and information retrieval systems
5. The role of human factors in the design of IT systems
6. Tools and techniques for the design and development of applications
7. Methods for the construction of web-based materials and systems, design of internet-based systems
8. Legal, professional and moral aspects of the exploitation of IT
9. Broad context within IT such as quality, reliability, enterprise, employment law, accounting and health
10. Challenges inherent in maintenance and evolution of IT-based systems, and techniques and best practices for dealing with them

---

## 2. Intellectual Skills

The IT graduate should additionally be able to:

1. Address IT systems problems, set goals towards solving them, observe results, reason and apply judgment
2. Identify attributes, components, relationships, patterns, main ideas, and errors
3. Summarize proposed solutions and their results
4. Restrict solution methodologies upon their results
5. Establish criteria, and verify solutions
6. Identify a range of solutions and critically evaluate and justify proposed design solutions
7. Solve IT problems with pressing commercial or industrial constraints
8. Generate an innovative design to solve a problem containing commercial and industrial constraints
9. Perform problem analysis from written descriptions; derive requirements specifications from an understanding of problems
10. Create and/or justify designs to satisfy given requirements (synthesis, evaluation, application)
11. Recognize the professional, moral and ethical issues involved in IT exploitation and reflect on issues of professional practice

---

## 3. Professional and Practical Skills

The IT graduate should additionally be able to:

1. Specify, investigate, analyze, design and develop computer-based systems using appropriate tools and techniques
2. Evaluate systems in terms of their quality and possible tradeoffs; evaluate appropriate hardware and software solutions for given scenarios
3. Recognize risks or safety aspects involved in the operation of computer-based systems
4. Deploy tools for the implementation and documentation of computer-based systems
5. Work as part of a development team and recognize the different roles of its members
6. Operate computing equipment efficiently, taking into account its logical and physical properties
7. Recognize and address professional, moral and ethical issues within the discipline
8. Effectively employ information-retrieval skills (using browsers, search engines, and on-line library catalogues); communicate effectively using a variety of methods; communicate effectively with team members, managers and customers
9. Make effective use of general IT facilities; plan and manage a project to complete within budget and schedule
10. Manage one's own learning and development, including time management and organizational skills
11. Present work in the form of reports, oral presentations or an internet web site
