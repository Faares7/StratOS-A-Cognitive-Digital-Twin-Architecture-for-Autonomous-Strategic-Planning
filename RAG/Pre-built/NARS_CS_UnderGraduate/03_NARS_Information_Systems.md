# NARS for Information Systems (IS)
## NAQAAE | Computing and Information, 1st Edition, October 2010

> Standards below are **in addition to** the General Computing and Information NARS.

---

## Introduction

Information Systems focuses on integrating information technology solutions and business processes to meet the information needs of businesses and other enterprises. IS views technology as an instrument for generating, processing, and distributing information.

**Four Core Areas of the IS Profession:**
1. IS professionals must have a **broad business and real-world perspective** — IS are enablers of organizational performance, spanning all levels and functions
2. IS professionals must have **strong analytical and critical thinking skills** — problem solving using systems concepts
3. IS professionals must exhibit **strong ethical principles** and have good interpersonal communication and team skills
4. IS professionals must **design and implement IT solutions** that enhance organizational performance

The IS specialist plays a key role in determining requirements for an organization's information systems and is active in their specification, design, and implementation.

---

## Graduate Attributes

After completing an Information Systems program, the graduate should be able to:

1. Recognize problems amenable to computer information systems and know the tools necessary for solving them
2. Understand fundamentals of SDLC, information networks, information security, data mining, e-commerce, geographical information systems, and crisis management
3. Manage and exploit organizational data and information; design data and information models; manage IS development resources and projects
4. Implement solutions using appropriate programming languages, web-based systems and tools, design methodologies, and database systems
5. Apply principles of effective information management, organization, mining, and retrieval to text, images, sound, and video
6. Know the fundamentals of intelligent information systems technologies
7. Specify, design, and implement computer-based information systems, and evaluate them in terms of quality attributes and tradeoffs
8. Apply IS solutions to functional, inter-organizational, operational, managerial, and executive problems
9. Describe characteristics of various IS components; use appropriate tools and techniques to analyze, design, and construct IS
10. Communicate effectively by oral, written and visual means
11. Work effectively as an individual and as a member of a team
12. Perform independent and efficient time management
13. Be aware of key ethical issues affecting information systems

## 1. Knowledge and Understanding

The IS graduate should additionally acquire knowledge and understanding of:

1. A core of analysis, algebra, applied mathematics and statistics
2. Information systems, data and information management, enterprise architecture, IS project management, IT infrastructure, systems analysis and design, and IS strategies
3. Principles and techniques of: database management systems, data mining, geographical information systems, multimedia, application development, business process management, enterprise systems, human-computer interaction, object-oriented analysis and design, e-technologies, image processing, information and infrastructure security, and computer graphics
4. Issues such as quality, reliability, enterprise, employment law, accounting and health
5. Organizational, human and economic sides of modern organizations
6. Principles of information communication and information security
7. Specification, analysis, design, implementation, operation and maintenance of IS solutions
8. Modeling organizational processes and data, defining and implementing technical and process solutions, managing projects, and integrating systems
9. Types and alternatives of global information systems architectures and their implications for organizational support

---

## 2. Intellectual Skills

The IS graduate should additionally be able to:

1. Define traditional and nontraditional IS problems, set goals towards solving them, and observe results
2. Perform comparisons between methods, techniques, etc.
3. Identify attributes, components, relationships, patterns, main ideas, and errors
4. Restrict solution methodologies upon their results
5. Select suitable tools, methods and techniques for modeling, analyzing IS, establishing criteria, and verifying solutions
6. Identify a range of solutions and critically evaluate and justify proposed design solutions
7. Solve IS problems with pressing commercial, time, and industrial constraints
8. Suggest an innovative design to solve a problem containing commercial and industrial constraints
9. Perform problem analysis from written descriptions; derive requirements specifications from an understanding of problems

---

## 3. Professional and Practical Skills

The IS graduate should additionally be able to:

1. Use appropriate programming languages, web-based systems and tools, design methodologies, and database systems
2. Use quantitative analysis techniques appropriately
3. Justify technological, methodological and management choices for an IS project for a given organization
4. Plan and manage an information systems project from inception to final implementation and cut-over
5. Produce acceptable reports and technical and user system documentation
6. Perform information acquisition and management using scientific literature and Web sources
7. Apply principles of effective information acquisition, management, organization, and retrieval to text, images, sound, and video
8. Apply principles of human-computer interaction to the evaluation and construction of user interfaces, web pages, and multimedia systems
9. Use tools to automate IS development phases
10. Analyze and document the feasibility of various options and compare solution options
11. Maintain existing information systems
