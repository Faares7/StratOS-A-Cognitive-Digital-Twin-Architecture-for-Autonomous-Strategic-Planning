# NARS for Computer Science (CS)
## NAQAAE | Computing and Information, 1st Edition, October 2010

> Standards below are **in addition to** the General Computing and Information NARS.

---

## Introduction

Computer Science spans a wide range from its theoretical and algorithmic foundations to World Wide Web applications, computer vision, intelligent systems, bioinformatics, high performance computing, distributed systems, object-oriented programming, grid and cloud computing.

**The work of computer scientists falls into three categories:**
- Design and implement software
- Supervise other programs by keeping them aware of new approaches
- Devise new ways to use computers

CS researchers develop the best possible ways to store information in databases, send data over networks, and display complex images. Their theoretical background allows them to determine the best performance possible, and their study of algorithms helps them develop new approaches.

---

## Graduate Attributes

After completing a Computer Science program, the graduate should be able to:

1. Demonstrate knowledge and competence in fundamental CS areas: algorithms design and analysis, computational theory, computer architecture and software-based systems
2. Apply mathematical foundations, algorithmic principles, and CS theory in modeling, design, implementation, evaluation and evolution of computer-based systems
3. Apply knowledge of mathematics and science to real-world problems; analyze and interpret data
4. Demonstrate the analytic skills necessary to effectively evaluate the relative merits of software, computer systems, and algorithmic approaches
5. Understand and apply a wide range of software engineering principles and tools such as design methodologies, algorithm choice, language, software libraries and user interface technique
6. Understand and apply principles and tools of natural language processing and data mining
7. Have a solid understanding of CS concepts to pursue further learning as graduate students or independently
8. Demonstrate understanding of algorithms and data structures, computer organization and architecture, programming language concepts, compilers, networks, AI, graphics, HCI, and databases
9. Design, implement, and evaluate a computer-based system, process, component or program
10. Use knowledge in modeling and design of computer-based systems demonstrating comprehension of tradeoffs in design choices

---

## 1. Knowledge and Understanding

The CS graduate should additionally acquire knowledge and understanding of:

1. The essential mathematics relevant to computer science
2. High-level programming languages
3. A core of analysis, algebra, applied mathematics and statistics
4. Interpreting and analyzing data qualitatively and/or quantitatively
5. Principles and techniques of application areas informed by research directions: artificial intelligence, natural language processing, data mining, databases and computer graphics
6. Critical understanding of the principles of artificial intelligence, image, and pattern recognition
7. Fundamental topics in CS: hardware and software architectures, software engineering principles and methodologies, operating systems, compilers, parallel and distributed computing, systems and software tools
8. Advanced topics for deeper understanding: hardware systems design, object-oriented analysis and design, artificial intelligence, parallel and concurrent computing

---

## 2. Intellectual Skills

The CS graduate should additionally be able to:

1. Define traditional and nontraditional problems, set goals towards solving them, and observe results
2. Perform comparisons between algorithms, methods, techniques, etc.
3. Perform classifications of data, results, methods, techniques, algorithms, etc.
4. Identify attributes, components, relationships, patterns, main ideas, and errors
5. Summarize proposed solutions and their results
6. Restrict solution methodologies upon their results
7. Establish criteria, and verify solutions
8. Identify a range of solutions and critically evaluate and justify proposed design solutions
9. Solve CS problems with pressing commercial or industrial constraints
10. Generate an innovative design to solve a problem containing commercial and industrial constraints

---

## 3. Professional and Practical Skills

The CS graduate should additionally be able to:

1. Use appropriate programming languages, web-based systems and tools, design methodologies, and knowledge and database systems
2. Communicate effectively by oral, written and visual means
3. Perform independent information acquisition and management using scientific literature and Web sources
4. Prepare and present seminars to a professional standard
5. Prepare technical reports and a dissertation to a professional standard; use IT skills and display mature computer literacy
6. Specify, design, and implement computer-based systems
7. Evaluate systems in terms of general quality attributes and possible tradeoffs presented within the given problem
8. Apply principles of effective information management, organization, and retrieval to text, images, sound, and video
9. Apply principles of human-computer interaction to evaluation and construction of user interfaces, web pages, and multimedia systems
10. Identify any risks or safety aspects involved in the operation of computing equipment
11. Deploy effectively the tools used for the construction and documentation of software, with emphasis on understanding the whole process involved in using computers to solve practical problems
12. Prepare technical reports and a dissertation to a professional standard
